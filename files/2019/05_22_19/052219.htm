Wizard of Oz
   Well, it's tornado season again. I've been a local Oklahoma resident all my life so I've kind of been normalized to it. It's a strange feeling to have danger looming overhead while you watch and wait. I got lucky and got the day off work to prepare and monitor the storms. My house was turned into a miniature command center, impromptu but data was flowing in from all over. There was a FM radio in the background tuned in to a station reporting on the warnings, my android phone was relaying texts to family and friends, and both computers were hopping websites to track radar. We had some close tornados in neighboring towns but nothing definitely coming our way.
   It's actually become easier to ready yourself against dangerous weather with the advent of smartphones and their emergency messages. Professional radio and weather websites aren't the only way to track storms anymore either. Amateur meteorologists like David Schlotthauer and MrMBB333 are great sources and often have livestreams during serious weather. Having experience with the tools and software gives them incredible insights and I've gleaned much from both of them. Along with watching reports from other people, you can also get the information directly from NEXRAD. Several programs exist but the ones I found to be the most intuitive are GR2Analyst and RadarScope. GR2Analyst has a 21-day trial available for download and the full license is available for a whopping $250... RadarScope on the other hand has a price that makes it more available to the layman, $9.99 for the Android and IOS apps and $29.99 for Windows and Mac computers. This doesn't unlock every feature though, it also has two subscription plans, Pro Tier 1 and Pro Tier 2. Pro Tier 1 is $9.99 per year and Pro Tier 2 is $99.99 per year or $14.99 per month. Neither Pro Tiers are needed and just add more advanced features. The basic version of RadarScope will usually suffice.
   The most important thing you can do is be prepared. Have a plan and keep up-to-date. Remain calm and think rationally, this isn't the time to rush off on a whim, poor decisions endanger you the most.

Itty Bitty Fi Billy
   About a month ago, I signed up for Google Fi. Formerly known as Project Fi, it offers cell phone plans at pretty reasonable rates. Piggybacking off the Sprint, T-Mobile and US Cellular networks, Google provides unlimited texts and calls for $20 per line and $10/Gb of data. After 6 gigabytes, the data becomes unlimited and you're only charged for what you use. So, half a gigabyte is $5, 250Mb is $2.⁵⁰ and so on. Really, not too bad a deal. You can see why I was interested.
   I made sure to get a promotional code to begin my service, which offers $20 off my bill after 30 days. There's lists of people sharing theirs everywhere. Of course, I found mine on Reddit. Ordered a Moto G6 at a discounted $100 to replace the loaner phone I was using, it was on it's last leg and wouldn't support a lot of the apps I was wanting. A mid-range phone is about all I needed, I don't play many mobile games, mostly just Pokemon GO from time to time.
   To start off with, FedEx shipped it to the wrong house and I got some lady's Slimfast instead. I followed the address on the shipment I received and had a trade with a stranger. Just a little inconvenience... Next, I activated the phone that night so I'd have it ready for tomorrow as my daily driver. Turns out Google will charge you a full month, $20 plus tax, for a few hours of activation. Just a little irritating... Beyond that, I've had a couple issues with MMS errors while sending images, on 'resend' it sends it twice to recipients. I've seen a couple of sim card errors, probably from switching towers, but they resolve themselves pretty quickly. All in all, nothing major. I've had the same problems on Verizon networks.
   I've had a Droid 4, Droid Turbo, and now this Moto G6. All of which fit my needs at the time and had their own unique faults. With the Droid 4, I delved into my first smart phone, learned about rooting and the Android OS. After a while, the slide out keyboard started wearing out and I had a hard time pressing buttons on it... So I upgraded to the flagship Droid Turbo. I learned to deal with the lack of a microSD slot, replacing a non-removable battery, and bypassing SafetyNet so I could play Pokemon GO on a rooted device. Eventually, it wore out as well, randomly shut down and had no working power button. I'd have to plug it into my laptop and use ADB and fastboot to bypass the broken button. This wasn't reliable enough for emergencies... I kept each of these phones well past their expected life, the Turbo lasted a whopping 4 years but it took quite a bit of tinkering... Whichever phone you get, learn and adapt to make the most of.
   As for Google Fi, there's been a lot of love and hate in online communities. Instead of including everyone's opinion, I figured I should just share my own. I don't want the latest phones or the newest features. I use wifi everywhere I go and live in an area with strong reception. How does Google Fi work for me? Perfectly fine, so far. My bill for my first full month was $5.¹⁴, not too bad, even if I did get $20 off.

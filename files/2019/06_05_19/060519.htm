Hai Kazuma Desu
   So you've watched an anime on Netflix and actually, kinda liked it... Maybe you've been a fan for a long time but find it difficult to find anything new. Well, good news. I'm a grandmaster otaku. As far back as I can remember, I've always loved the unique storytelling. I've consumed countless hours of anime and manga, and delved into visual novels and more obscure interests.
   I could name-drop shows and characters but everyone has their own preferences and it usually devolves into waifu-wars and nothing productive ever comes from that. Instead, I would like to talk about where to find the latest information. To start with, Reddit's r/Anime and r/Manga are great communities. I've found several recommendations from both subreddits that I would have ignored otherwise. Sometimes unflattering blurbs or screenshots from previews will make me write off a show. There's just too many shows to keep up with every season and sometimes they just don't align with my interests. You don't have to watch every show to be a fan of anime.
   Japan broadcasts shows seasonally. Spring, summer, fall and winter. By the time it's ready for the english audience there are two forms to watch, the subtitled version and the dubbed version. Dubbing english audio takes quite a bit longer to produce and will often result in suboptimal localizations. Having the wrong voice actors or mistranslations can be jarring to the immersion and can break the context of stories. This isn't always the case but it happens frequently...
   Translating, adding subtitles and leaving the original audio is my preferred way to watch most anime. It can take a while to pick up on situations or scenes that rely heavily on cultural aspects but the punchlines are delivered in a more fluid and complete fashion. When the show is currently airing and translated professionally it's call a simulcast. Subtitles done unofficially are called fansubs. When a show becomes licensed in your country it is illegal to download through unofficial channels.
   While it is best to support companies that distribute licensed and legal anime, like Crunchyroll or VRV, there are times when the show you want to watch isn't provided officially. The alternative I have used in the past is Nyaa Torrents but it has been shut down and relocated domains several times... It keeps popping back up in new incarnations though.
   Keeping up with the influx of new shows each season can be a real chore. Random Curiosity is a great place to find seasonal previews, schedules for airing dates and discussions on episodes aired. The breakdowns they do are detailed and insightful but they can't cover every show for each season though. The ones that are picked are arguably the best of that season. Other places to find information on anything Otaku related would be My Anime List and Sankaku Complex.
   Anime can be an original story or adapted from alternate forms of media, most commonly from mangas or light novels. Recently the trend of adapting from mobile games has become nearly as common. When I don't have enough time to fully digest an episode, or I've run out of shows I want to watch, reading manga has always been my fallback plan. Reading manga on a phone is my preferred method. In the past, I read directly from website until I found apps for Android to be more user-friendly. Originally I used an app called MangaRock. It was work perfectly fine, even after it was removed from Google's Play Store. I used it on my first two Android phones, until they started selling wallpapers... My current favorite app is Tachiyomi. It does everything I liked about MangaRock but is open source and has no in-app purchases. Tachiyomi also has a subreddit at r/Tachiyomi I would recommend, especially if you're having trouble using it.
   Another way to get your anime fix is through visual novels. While not strictly anime or manga related anymore, the genre originally came from Japan and the majority are still anime themed. Steam is probably the best source but has had an issue with censoring content from the games or completely banning games from being sold... There are ways around this and from the usually sources, it just takes a little google-fu.

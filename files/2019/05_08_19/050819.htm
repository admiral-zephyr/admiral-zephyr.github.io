Feather the Edges
   This is software everyone should have installed and ready to use. GIMP, or GNU Image Manipulation Program, is basically the free version of Photoshop. Everyone's heard of Photoshop, it's a verb nowadays, like Google, but not everyone knows how to use it. For some, it's a bit like witchcraft, and that shouldn't be the case. Getting started takes almost no effort, just download and install it.
   Working with GIMP is fairly easy, there's always a tutorial out there to show you the steps needed, but a lot of it is just experimenting. Trial and error. A good place to get started would be GIMP's own tutorial page. There's also a multitude of other websites and Youtube videos that offer guides. Besides altering images, GIMP is also very powerful at manipulating files. Combining PDFs or a series of images into a GIF is pretty straightforward though it may take some time to learn how to accomplish these tasks quickly. Working with the layers is key.
   After a while you'll start adding tricks to your repertoire. One of my favorites is adding a glow to objects. 1) Duplicate the layer you want to add a glow to. 2) Make the duplicate image all one color, the color you want the glow to be. 3) Under 'Filters' then 'Blur' select 'Gaussian Blur'. Follow the prompt and make sure the glow layer is underneath the original image. If you want a more intense glow, copy and paste the glow layer until it reaches the desired effect. You should have a pretty nice glow now. You can even make a black glow, offset the position under the original and tweak the transparency with 'Opacity' and end up with a simple shadow effect.
   You don't have to use GIMP to make effects or be any kind of artistic. It's just a great program and a much better solution than MS Paint.

Party Time! Excellent!
   I've dabbled in streaming a little bit and I had a buddy bring it up so I thought I'd cover OBS Studio this time. I don't have a regular schedule for Twitch streaming but it's a great way to show off a game to a friend on the fence about buying it. There's plenty of alternatives to OBS Studio, like Nvidia's Shadowplay, but this one is free and works on Windows, Mac and Linux.
   While I don't have a wealth of knowledge on the subject, there's plenty of resources to help you along. On install, there's an automated wizard to help you set up whether you want to stream or save to your hard drive to edit and upload later. Other programs, like GIMP and VLMC, can help increase the production value.
   The program itself is pretty straightforward. A preview window up top. Scenes on the left are a collection of sources to the right. You can hotkey scene transitions to make afk messages. Sources are layers to the video, things like game windows, webcams and watermarks. In the middle is the sound levels, and on the right is the controls and settings.
   There's plenty of ways to use OBS but make sure to be safe about it. Don't share anything illegal like copyrighted material. Be careful with sensitive data like credit cards or social security numbers. Honestly, avoiding actual names is a pretty good idea too. And read the rules of the video provider you're hosting to. Once somethings online, it's kinda there forever.

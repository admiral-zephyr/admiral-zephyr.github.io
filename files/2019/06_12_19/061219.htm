You're Breathtaking
   It's that time of year again! Every gamer is on the edge of their seats waiting for the latest news on their favorite console, series and genre of game. The Electronic Entertainment Expo, or E3, is the largest event for the video game industry. There's already tons of people covering this. Breaking down and analyzing every snippet and sound bite. The best I can do is talk about what I'm looking forward to most.
   Cyberpunk 2077 is probably the number one game everyone is looking at this year, especially with Keanu Reeves taking the stage to introduce it. Set in the future, as the title suggests, it's a gritty glimpse of a future similar to Bladerunner or Deus Ex. The atmosphere of the game is breathtaking. It's dark and foreboding with a fluid and immersive style of gameplay.
   You play as a mercenary outlaw in a megalopolis called Night City looking for an implant that's the key to immortality. The game will be an open-world action-adventure with FPS and RPG mechanics. The game is set to release April 16th, 2020.
   The first Baldur's Gate came out in 1998 and Baldur's Gate II was released in 2000. The Baldur's Gate series is quite renowned and there's been expansions and remastered editions but Baldur's Gate III has now been officially announced, again. It was canceled over a decade ago when Interplay forfeited the D&D PC license to Atari.
   The trailer featured a knight in red and gray violently transforming into what appears to be a mindflayer. It will be set 100 years after the events in Baldur's Gate II. It will be using a modified version of the Dungeon and Dragons 5th Edition ruleset and there will be a pen and paper prequel to usher in Baldur's Gate III but no release date has been announced yet.
   The hype for this game has been building ever since Nintendo's Switch was announced. While we have had Pokemon games on the Switch, this will be the first time we get the full Pokemon experience with an original game and not just a remake or adaptation, like Let's GO Eevee and Let's GO Pikachu. Everyone is already analyzing every nugget of information and speculating every imaginable outcome.
   The new Pokemon takes place on the continent of Galar, which seems to have a British influence this time. There will be a new version of the Pokemon Bank called Pokemon Home, but not every pokemon will be transferable to Sword and Shield... Instead of Mega Evolutions and Z-Moves they've decided to try something called Dynamax where you can turn your pokemon into titanic monsters and also adds a raiding feature to take down Dynemaxed pokemon. Releasing November 15th, 2019.
   There's quite a few more I didn't cover like Link's Awakening, the Final Fantasy 7 Remake and Fire Emblem. And there's also many others that looks promising this year. All in all this E3 was pretty respectable.

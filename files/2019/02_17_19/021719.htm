Staying Busy with Nothing
   The holidays are pretty much over and I can't wait for things to slow down. My schedule has been filled with appointments and obligations, seemingly unending, and I could do with some downtime to replenish my sanity. Besides upcoming dentist visits, I also have an interview for jury duty looming on the horizon. Hopefully, by the time warmer weather hits, I'll be done with these inane tasks and I can focus on fixing the Ranchero. It has several mechanical issues I need to work on and I can't wait to drive it again.
   Even with my overloaded itinerary, I have had an opportunity to play a few games in my downtime. The bulk of my which has been Fortnite. I got 'Save the World' on sale and I've been quite impressed with the pve side. The free pvp mode was alright but I've never really been into the solo, duo or squad queues and stick mostly to the team vs team mode. The missions range from collecting items to defense and escort type missions, but the best part of the pve mode is managing your gear and crafting. Unlike the pvp mode, you pick the weapons you'll take on the mission. I recommend getting 'Save the World' for Fortnite but not at full price.
   I've also been getting into a few retro games. SWGEmu and FortressOne are some great throwbacks to get back into, or start fresh if you missed them in their glory days. Star Wars Galaxies launched on June 26, 2003. The crafting system was amazing for its time but later content would break what many enjoyed and the servers closed for good on December 15, 2011. Before this happened though, much of the community had come together and made their own server and rolled the content back to 'Pre-CU' as it became known. The community server, SWGEmu, has been up since and regularly tops a thousand online players.
   Fortress One is a standalone version of the Quake 1 mod, Team Fortress, that uses nquake to install and update, and ezquake as the modified quakeworld engine. There are fewer people here but it still feels active for a mod of a game that came out in 1996. And if Team Fortress isn't your thing, theres also the standard Quake 1 from Quakeworld.

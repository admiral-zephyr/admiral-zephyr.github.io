Big Ape is All Seeing
   Chucklefish quickly became one of my favorite indie developers when it released Starbound, a 2D platformer with survival and crafting mechanics. It has retro styled graphics, the usual pixel sprites just like the majority of all the other indie games out there. What makes Starbound special isn't the story or the mechanics but the personality of the game itself. Dialog and descriptions bring the characters and world to life.
   Most sandbox games have little to no story. It's entirely up to the player to come up with a reason to progress through the game. I would rather see a complete lack of story than be forced to play through a bad one and while Starbound doesn't have a mind-blowing epic narrative, it does well enough to keep me interested while pushing the plot along.
   The main story of Starbound is pretty straightforward. At your graduation ceremony for the Protectorate, Earth is destroyed by a giant space tentacle called the Ruin. Your character manages to escape and you start the game fixing your spaceship. You eventually visit the civilizations of other races while searching for clues about the Ruin while helping each of the races overcome their own obstacles. This can be skipped as well. You can take off and explore the immense universe full of planets and moons, space stations and starships.
   The character creation gives you plenty of ways to customize. Firstly, there's race. You can be Human, Apex, Floran, Avian, Hylotl, Glitch and Novakid. Each with a unique cultural theme, armor sets and ships. Humans lost Earth so they tend to have campsite and RVs, more of a modern nomad theme. Apex are inteligent apes with a dystopian society straight out of 1984. Florans are humanoid plants that are tribal and have cannibalistic tendencies. Avians are bird people and heavily influenced by the ancient egyptians. Hylotl are bipedal fish folk with a panache for Japanese culture. Glitch are medieval robots complete with castles and kings. Novakid are star-people with a hankering for the wild west. After that, you can choose your gender, hair type, hair color, outfit and personality type.
   Gameplay consists of exploring environments of different habitability across the universe and gathering supplies to craft upgrades and build bases. The player is given a lot of block types and placeables to build with. This is where Starbound truly shines. Building bases is taken to grand heights and not just because you can build your own space station. With a little creativity you can build about anything. Other than the main quest, there's also a multitude of side quests ranging from upgrading abilities to acquiring mechs to fly in space. Starbound also features a massive amount of collectible items like pets, action figures, fish, bugs, fossils and cooking recipes.
   Recently Starbound received the long-awaited Bounty Hunter update! You can now hunt down gangs of criminals across the galaxies for the Peacekeepers. There are procedural missions, new elemental monsters, new weapons and armors, new furniture, and tons of other things. This also adds a new boss encounter, the Swansong. It's been about two years since Starbound received it's Spacefarer update and even I was wondering if this game was finished. The community remained fairly active however, making mods for Starbound and posting builds of the week on Reddit. Seeing patch 1.4 sparked some hope for this great game. Hopefully this isn't the last we hear of it and I really hope it comes to Nintendo like Stardew Valley did as well, but that's a long shot for now.
